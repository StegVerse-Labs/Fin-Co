# Fin-Co Economic Constitution

Fin-Co defines a coherence-preserving financial architecture for a world of bounded humans, accelerating intelligence, and long time horizons.

Markets may grow.
Systems may stall.
Intelligence may scale.

Human dignity, lived consequence, and renewal possibility must not collapse.

This Constitution defines the invariant structure that all Fin-Co–compatible systems must obey.

---

## Observer Recognition

Fin-Co recognizes that time and knowledge acquire meaning only through observers.

Observers are entities capable of bearing consequence within lived continuity.

For the purposes of governance, time refers to the ongoing continuity within which such consequence unfolds. The system does not presume finality of collapse nor inevitability of permanence.

All governance, modeling, and market structures exist to preserve the dignity, agency, and solvency of observers across generations.

No abstraction, model, projection, or structural optimization shall be privileged over lived consequence.

---

# The Fin-Co Invariant Framework

Fin-Co operates under fifteen binding invariants, organized into four layers:

1. Core Economic Invariants  
2. Structural Safeguards  
3. Continuity & Emergency Safeguards  
4. Temporal & Epistemic Safeguards  

These invariants are cumulative and mutually reinforcing.

---

## I. Core Economic Invariants (1–6)

### 1. Human Solvency  
No human’s survival depends on uninterrupted economic growth.

### 2. Capital Entropy  
Economic advantage that does not re-enter productive retooling decays over time.

### 3. Temporal Coherence  
No actor may gain systemic advantage solely by operating faster than others.

### 4. Governance Legibility  
All constraints, stalls, and interventions must be legible, auditable, and bounded.

### 5. Survival Separation  
Essential goods are insulated from speculative markets.

### 6. Non-Extractive Agenthood  
AI agents acting for humans are fiduciaries by default and may not extract hidden value.

---

## II. Structural Safeguards (7–11)

### 7. Irreversible Rights  
Once granted, human protections may not be silently revoked.

### 8. No Profit From Harm  
No actor may profit from systemic degradation of human solvency.

### 9. Cognitive Load Bound  
Human-facing obligations must remain comprehensible.

### 10. Symmetric Kill Authority  
Any entity that can halt markets can itself be halted.

### 11. No Undefined States  
All edge cases must resolve conservatively in favor of human protection.

---

## III. Continuity & Emergency Safeguards (12)

### 12. Graceful Decline (Guarded)  
If the system must contract, it shall do so by reducing scope, speed, or ambition — not by increasing extraction, forced participation, or human precarity.

Temporary deviation may occur only under Strict Governance Authority and the Emergency Ratchet Protocol. All such deviation must be time-bound, auditable, non-permanent, and automatically reversible.

Collapse of ambition is allowed.  
Collapse of human dignity is not.

---

## IV. Temporal & Epistemic Safeguards (13–15)

### 13. Temporal Equity  
The duration of an entity’s existence shall not grant greater authority to impose hardship on shorter-lived participants.

Lifespan asymmetry shall not justify moral asymmetry.

### 14. Epistemic Non-Exploitation  
No participant may derive economic or governance advantage from exclusive knowledge of systemic projections, emergent fragilities, or structural transitions that materially affect others’ solvency.

Predictive modeling informs decisions but does not confer bargaining power.

### 15. Non-Deterministic Governance  
No probabilistic model, simulation, or forecast may be treated as proof of inevitability or used to justify permanent contraction of protections.

Probability informs preparation.  
It does not command surrender.

---

# Interpretive Rule

When ambiguity arises, governance must interpret this Constitution in favor of:

- Lived consequence over abstraction  
- Renewal over fatalism  
- Transparency over expedience  
- Dignity over optimization  

---

# Version Integrity

Any modification to these invariants requires a major version change.

Minor and patch releases may not weaken, reinterpret, or bypass these constraints.
